What is Biology?

Biology is a type of science that studies life.

Origin

The origin of biology can be traced back to two ancient Greek men known as Hippocrates of Kos and Galen of Pergamum. Hippocrates of Kos was a medical researcher, while Galen of Pergamum was a researcher in anatomy and physiology. Information about medicine and natural history became extremely popular in the middle ages. A large amount of Islamic students would study medicine, while natural history was used as philosophy to the Aristotelians. As the years passed, these two studies were incorporated in one main separate subject known as biology.
