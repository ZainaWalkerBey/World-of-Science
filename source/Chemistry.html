What is Chemistry?


Chemistry is a type of science that studies the identification and behavior of matter.

Origin

The origin of chemistry can be traced to a man by the name of Jābir ibn Hayyān. Around the eighth century, he discovered and wrote about different types of ideas such as, distillation, crystallization, sublimation and evaporation. He also invented many useful tools such as, the alembic. An alembic is used to study acids.

Chemistry started to become more prevalent in Europe, when alchemists wanted to use the ideas behind chemistry to turn different metals into gold or silver. Alchemists also want to use a combination of chemicals in hopes of creating a solution that would invent immortality. Due to many the many laws that goes with chemistry, they were not able to accomplish these desires.
